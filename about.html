About Me


I am a first generation college student from a small town in West Tennessee called Henderson. I first discovered my love for computers and technology at a young age, playing video games and using the family desktop (mostly for MS Paint). Growing up, my access to the internet was limited to my time at school, so almost the entirety of my computer knowledge comprised of self taught trial-and-error. I was fascinated by how computers were so prevelant in everyday life, and strived to learn as much as I could about them.
My fascination with computers culminated when I made the decision to pursue a bachelor's degree in Computer Science at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. Prior to my first day of class, I had absolutely no experience programming. Since then, I have learned and used nearly a dozen computer languages, and many more technologies.
I am currently a full time student, and am available for part time work. I plan to graduate May 2017, after which I will be available for a full time position.
The majority of my experience has been with the C language. C++ was the first language I learned in school, and have since learned and used Python, Java, Javascript, R, Perl, PHP, Go, and Lisp. I have most enjoy working with Python, and indeed one of the largest projects I have yet worked on was written in Python.
I enjoy web programming more than perhaps any other style of programming, as it mixes my love for programming with my love for art and design. I like the process of thinking through how to lay out elements in ways that make sense, and in ways that make people's lives easier.
